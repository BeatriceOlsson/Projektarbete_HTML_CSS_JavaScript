Carry on, Jeeves

Förfatare: P.G.Wodehouse

Antal sidor: 304

Omdöme: 9/10

Utgivardatum: 2018-06-28

Länk till Adlibris

Beskrivning

In Carry On, Jeeves, P.G. Wodehouse introduces readers to the delightful world of Bertie Wooster and his extraordinary valet, Jeeves. This collection of ten brilliantly crafted short stories follows Bertie—a lovable but hapless young gentleman—and Jeeves, whose razor-sharp intellect and impeccable timing are the antidote to every sticky situation Bertie finds himself in.
From thwarting disastrous engagements to outsmarting meddlesome aunts and solving romantic tangles for Bertie’s equally bumbling friends, Jeeves’s genius is unmatched. Whether ghostwriting letters for a bohemian artist, breaking ill-advised betrothals, or concocting schemes to win the favor of overbearing relatives, Jeeves always has a plan.
Each story is brimming with Wodehouse’s trademark humor, clever wordplay, and absurdly entertaining scenarios. Bertie’s narrative voice—witty, exaggerated, and charmingly clueless—provides the perfect counterpoint to Jeeves’s calm and composed demeanor. Together, their dynamic is a comedic masterstroke, creating a world where life’s mishaps become a source of endless amusement.
Whether it’s The Artistic Career of Corky, where Jeeves attempts to help an artist win his uncle’s approval, or Clustering Round Young Bingo, featuring Bertie’s hopelessly romantic friend and his latest misadventure, every tale in this collection is a gem. Jeeves, with his unerring sense of propriety and quiet brilliance, always emerges as the hero, rescuing Bertie and his friends with solutions as ingenious as they are hilarious.
Set in the whimsical world of British upper-class society, Carry On, Jeeves gently pokes fun at the idle rich while delivering timeless humor. With its sharply drawn characters, ingenious plots, and sparkling prose, this collection is perfect for fans of witty, lighthearted comedy. Step into the laugh-out-loud world of Bertie and Jeeves, where no predicament is too absurd, no scheme too complex, and no master-servant relationship quite as entertaining.
